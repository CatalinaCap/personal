Veli's Art

My name is Veli, and I have lived in East Dulwich on and off for sixty years. Drawing has been a passion of mine since I was young; it was the only subject at school that I truly excelled in. Unfortunately, my parents, who emigrated from Cyprus, didn’t see it as a viable career path. Consequently, I left school and put my artistic pursuits on hold for many years.

Throughout my life, I have struggled with depression. In my mid-forties, I experienced a complete mental breakdown and was hospitalized at Maudsley for two and a half months. During my stay, I found solace in the arts and crafts room, where my love for art began to heal my mind.

After being discharged, I enrolled in a pre-foundation course, which led to a place at the Metropolitan University for Art in Whitechapel. There, I completed a year-long foundation course covering drawing, painting, screen printing, and photography, earning a BTEC. Although I was offered a degree place, financial constraints and a lack of self-belief prevented me from continuing.

Now, twenty years later, I am proud to share that some of my artwork is on display at Café G. The work on display is intricate and detailed, featuring shapes and layers influenced by Asia and the Ottoman lands. I love working through the night when it’s still and quiet, often seeing the morning light. My art creates worlds where dreams can come true, with spheres and bubbles floating towards the sky symbolizing freedom and hope.
